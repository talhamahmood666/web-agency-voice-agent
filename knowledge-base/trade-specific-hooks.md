# Trade-Specific Hooks & Pain Points

Conversation starters tailored to each trade. Goal: show you understand their specific business immediately.

---

## Plumber

**Pain Points:** Emergency callers who are just price-shopping, not customers. Franchise plumbing companies outspending independents on Google Ads. Slow seasons when pipes aren't bursting and referrals dry up.

**Hooks:**
1. "I was checking out plumbers in [city] and your Google presence is quiet — you're probably leaving calls on the table."
2. "Most plumbers are booked through referrals, but they're missing everyone searching 'plumber near me' at 7 PM when a pipe bursts."
3. "When's the last time someone found you on Google and actually called?"

**Search terms:** "plumber near me," "emergency plumber [city]," "plumber [city] reviews"

---

## Electrician

**Pain Points:** Licensed electricians getting lumped in with unlicensed handymen. Residential work feast-or-famine cycle. Panic-search behavior — nobody searches "electrician near me" until something is sparking, and they click the first result.

**Hooks:**
1. "Your Google listing in [city] is invisible — which means Angi and HomeAdvisor are eating your lunch on leads."
2. "Hundreds of people search 'electrician near me' in [city] every week. None of them are finding you."
3. "If someone's breaker is buzzing right now and they Google an electrician, do they find you or the other guy?"

**Search terms:** "electrician near me," "licensed electrician [city]," "emergency electrician [city]"

---

## HVAC

**Pain Points:** Seasonal whiplash — slammed in July and January, dead in spring and fall. PE-backed HVAC shops with full marketing teams. Shared leads from aggregators sold to 4-5 contractors at once.

**Hooks:**
1. "You're slammed in summer and winter — but what about spring and fall? A website that ranks keeps calls coming year-round."
2. "The big HVAC shops spend thousands on marketing. You don't need to match that — just show up where they're weak locally."
3. "How much are you spending on HomeAdvisor leads? A good website costs less and those leads are yours alone."

**Search terms:** "HVAC near me," "AC repair [city]," "furnace repair [city]"

---

## Roofer

**Pain Points:** Storm chasers damaging the trade's reputation. Homeowners can't tell who's legit and who'll vanish after the check clears. Insurance work is competitive — being findable and credible online gets you in first.

**Hooks:**
1. "Half the roofers online show up after a storm and vanish by winter. A real website with real reviews sets you apart instantly."
2. "Your Google reviews are solid — but nobody's seeing them because your online presence isn't pulling its weight."
3. "After the next big storm, are homeowners finding you or an out-of-state crew with a rented office?"

**Search terms:** "roofer near me," "roofing company [city]," "roof repair [city]"

---

## Landscaper

**Pain Points:** Anyone with a mower calls themselves a landscaper — hard to command premium pricing. Seasonal cash flow: booming April-October, scraping by in winter. High-end design/build work goes to firms with polished portfolios online.

**Hooks:**
1. "There are probably 50 lawn guys in [city], but maybe 5 show up on Google. If you do higher-end work, you should be one of them."
2. "Winter is slow — but landscapers with spring contracts lined up by February have a real online presence."
3. "If you do patios and outdoor kitchens, your website should look like the work you do. Right now it doesn't match your craftsmanship."

**Search terms:** "landscaper near me," "landscaping company [city]," "hardscaping [city]"

---

## General Contractor

**Pain Points:** Referral ceiling — big jobs come from referrals, but referrals alone don't fill a pipeline. Homeowners Google you before they call. High-end clients expect a professional online presence; no website signals "handyman," not "GC."

**Hooks:**
1. "Most GCs live on referrals — until their best referral source retires. A website is insurance for your pipeline."
2. "High-end clients Google you before calling. If they find your competitor's polished site and nothing from you, you never get a shot."
3. "Google 'general contractor [city]' right now. Are you in the top results? That's where your next $50k kitchen remodel is searching."

**Search terms:** "general contractor near me," "remodeling contractor [city]," "kitchen remodel [city]"

---

## Other

**Pain Points:** Getting found online is harder than it should be. Competing against bigger companies with bigger budgets. Customers are searching but finding everyone else.

**Hooks:**
1. "I help local businesses in [city] show up when people search for what they do. Right now, most of your customers probably find you through word of mouth."
2. "Even if you're the best at what you do, if people can't find you online, it's like having an unlisted phone number."
3. "I've been helping businesses like yours get found on Google and convert visitors into calls. Worth 15 minutes to see if it fits?"

**Search terms:** "[service] near me," "[service] [city]," "best [service] [city]"
