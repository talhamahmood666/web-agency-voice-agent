# Competitor Comparison

This is internal reference for handling questions about alternatives. Keep the tone factual, not negative — don't trash competitors, just explain the differences honestly.

---

## Us vs. DIY (Wix / Squarespace / GoDaddy)

**What they offer:** Drag-and-drop website builders. Anyone can make a site for $15-$30/month.

**The reality:** Building the site is the easy part. Making it rank on Google, managing your Google Business Profile, writing content that targets the right keywords, and keeping everything updated month after month — that's the actual work. A DIY site that nobody finds is just a digital business card. Most trade business owners don't have time to learn SEO, write content, and manage a GBP. They should be on job sites, not in website builders.

**The bottom line:** DIY saves money upfront but costs way more in missed leads. A site that doesn't rank might as well not exist.

---

## Us vs. Big Agencies ($3,000-$8,000+)

**What they offer:** Full-service digital marketing — websites, SEO, PPC, social media, branding, content marketing.

**The reality:** Big agencies have big overhead — offices, account managers, sales teams. You pay for all of that. They also work across industries, which means their plumber client gets the same generic playbook as their dentist client. They charge premium prices because they need to, not because you get premium results.

**The bottom line:** We deliver the same website quality and SEO results at a fraction of the cost because we specialize. We know exactly what works for trades and we don't waste time (or your money) on things that don't.

---

## Us vs. "My Nephew / Buddy Does It"

**What they offer:** Someone who knows how to use WordPress or Wix builds you a site, often for free or cheap.

**The reality:** Building a site is one thing. Ongoing SEO, GBP management, content updates, performance monitoring, and review response management are a completely different skill set. Most friend-built sites sit untouched for years — no rankings, no updates, no leads. There's also no accountability: when your buddy gets busy with his real job, your website drops to the bottom of his priority list.

**The bottom line:** A website that doesn't actively generate leads isn't an asset — it's just a URL on your truck. Friendly doesn't equal effective.

---

## Us vs. Doing Nothing (Word of Mouth Only)

**What they offer:** No cost, no effort. Just rely on referrals.

**The reality:** Word of mouth works — until it doesn't. It has a hard ceiling. Your best referral source retires. A slow season hits and nobody's talking about you. Meanwhile, your competitors with websites and Google Business Profiles are getting found by people who don't have anyone to ask. Those are leads you'll never even know you lost.

**The bottom line:** A website isn't a replacement for referrals — it's insurance for when referrals aren't enough. It catches the customers who are searching right now, ready to hire, and deciding based on who shows up first on Google. If that's not you, it's your competitor.
